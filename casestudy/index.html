Project Overview

This capstone project goes over the future decline of American Infrastructure. The main goal of the project is to illustrate how if the government proceeds to carry on with current investment or lack of for these sectors of infrastructure, residents of the future will be doomed.

The project is in the format of a flipbook that is narrated with a sarcastic can do attitude that is inspired by the attitudes expressed in 1950s advertisements.

Project Research

I conducted my research through the internet so that I can get a grasp of the what the current state of America's infrastructure. Through this research I found that some of the most impacted major areas of infrastructure are public transportation, electric fuel adoption, and flight travel.

Visual Design

For the Visual Design aspect of my project I decided to lean to a more image centric approach since I am working with the flipbook medium. I made it so that viewers could have the experience of viewing a magazine collage while having text being fed to you without intruding on the images that are being flipped. Furthermore, I had the text content type out of screen so I could get the effect described in the latter sentence.

User Interface

The user interface begins when you go one scroll down from the titlescreen. You will be greeted with the book and an instruction that says to use the arrow keys to navigate through the book.

In the book you will expect the following interactive behaviors:

  1. Scroll down.
  2. Right arrow key to flip page.
  3. Left arrow key to flip back.

Revisions

For revisions, I added more interactive elements to the pages. For instance, I added a hover interaction in which you can get more facts through a scripted point. I also added some charts you can hover on to get more visuals on how dire the future looks.

Summary

After completing this project, I have come to reflect upon how I have improved and what I have learned from this experience. When I started this project, I was very unsure about my abilities to work with libraries and javascript. While working on the project, I became more comfortable with experimenting with javascript without caring about the code coming out right or not. I was able to embrace the challenges that were asked of me and I am proud to say that I am not only a better coder than before but also a better risk taker as well.
